Search, automate, protect, and share everything you copy.

Deck turns clipboard history into a working tool. Semantic search, AI assistance, paste queues, privacy controls, and LAN sharing — built into one macOS-native app.

Find anything you've copied

Describe what you remember and Deck finds the match — even days later. On-device semantic search, OCR text extraction, content recognition, and precision filters work together so you spend less time looking and more time working.

Scenario

You copied a database connection string from Slack three days ago. You only remember it had something to do with the staging server. Type that into Deck and semantic search surfaces the exact snippet in under 2 seconds — no scrolling, no asking your teammate to resend it.

Explore Semantic Search

Put AI to work on what you've copied

Deck AI connects to ChatGPT, Claude, or local Ollama models and works directly with your clipboard history. Search, summarize, rewrite, and automate — with explicit approval before any write or delete.

Scenario

You copied five competitor product descriptions throughout the morning. Open Deck AI and ask it to create a comparison table from today's clips. It finds all five, generates a formatted Markdown table, and waits for your approval before writing the result back to your clipboard.

Explore Deck AI

Queue it, paste it, done

Line up multiple clips in a Paste Queue and step through them in order. Smart Rules auto-tag, transform, or remove clips as they arrive. Context-Aware Ordering brings the right content type forward for each app.

Scenario

You need to fill a form with eight fields — name, email, phone, address, and more. Add all eight values to the Paste Queue, switch to the form, and press Cmd+Shift+V for each field. Every press pastes the next value in order. Done in 10 seconds instead of 2 minutes of app-switching.

Explore Queue Mode

Sensitive data stays on your Mac

Clipboard history lives on your device by default. Deck locks access with Touch ID, encrypts sensitive content, detects card numbers and passwords automatically, and hides itself during screen sharing.

Scenario

You're sharing your screen on a Zoom call and need to paste a password. Deck detects the screen recording and hides its window. After the call, Touch ID is required before clipboard history reappears. The password is already protected, and a card number from an invoice was flagged as sensitive automatically.

Explore Privacy Controls

Send clips between Macs over your own network

Deck connects your Macs directly over LAN — Bonjour discovery or manual IP. Transfers are encrypted and verified with a short-lived code. No account, no cloud relay, your data stays on your network.

Scenario

You have the right code snippet on your personal Mac and need it on your work machine. Open Deck on both, they find each other over Wi-Fi in seconds. Select the snippet, tap Share, confirm the 6-digit code on both sides, and the clip arrives encrypted — no cloud, no Apple ID required.
